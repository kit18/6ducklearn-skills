# Presentation Orchestrator Consulting Case Smoke Test

This is a synthetic, public-safe smoke fixture. It is not evidence about a real company.

## Messy Source Notes

- B2B SaaS company sells workflow automation to mid-market operations teams.
- Growth slowed in the last two quarters.
- Horizontal outbound still creates demos, but conversion is uneven and sales cycles are lengthening.
- Healthcare operations prospects show stronger urgency, but there is concern about compliance, implementation burden, and distracting the sales team.
- Leadership asks whether to invest in a 90-day vertical GTM motion or keep optimizing the horizontal motion.
- Available evidence is directional only: CRM notes, recent lost-deal themes, sales team feedback, and early healthcare prospect calls.

## Deck Brief

- Audience: CEO, Head of Sales, Head of Product, RevOps.
- Desired decision: Approve or reject a 90-day healthcare operations vertical GTM test.
- Presentation type: Executive decision deck.
- Time / slide target: 10 minutes, 12 slides.
- Source material: Synthetic messy notes above.
- Constraints: No fabricated customer quotes, market size, CAC, revenue, or compliance claims.
- Assumptions: Healthcare operations is a candidate vertical, not a proven segment.

## Route Card

- Decision job: Growth/GTM investment decision.
- Answer state: uncertain hypothesis with options tradeoff.
- Primary framework: McKinsey problem-solving spine.
- Support methods: Playing to Win strategy cascade, Business Model Canvas, Traction/Bullseye.
- Why this route: Leadership must decide where to play and how to test a vertical motion without overcommitting.
- Known evidence: directional CRM notes, sales feedback, early prospect calls.
- Critical gaps: segment economics, compliance needs, implementation effort, repeatable pain, channel economics.
- Output type: Consulting business storytelling deck.

## Consulting POV

- Recommendation or hypothesis: Approve a scoped 90-day healthcare operations vertical GTM test, not a full pivot.
- Why now: Horizontal growth is slowing while one vertical shows stronger urgency.
- Strategic logic: A narrow test can validate whether urgency, repeatability, and channel focus outweigh compliance and implementation risk.
- Value at stake: A sharper segment focus could improve sales efficiency and product messaging, but only if implementation and compliance burden stay manageable.
- Options considered: optimize horizontal motion, run vertical test, fully pivot to healthcare.
- Rejected paths: full pivot is premature; horizontal-only optimization ignores a directional urgency signal.
- Tradeoffs: focus improves message-market fit but reduces sales breadth during the test.
- Risks and mitigations: compliance ambiguity, product distraction, small sample bias, channel economics.
- Confidence: Medium-low until segment economics and implementation effort are tested.
- What would change our mind: healthcare sales cycle stays long, required compliance work blocks deals, or early pipeline does not show higher conversion quality.
- Decision ask: Approve a 90-day test with explicit gates, not permanent repositioning.

## Problem-Solving Spine

- Problem type: restore current state and pursue ideal state.
- Issue question: Should leadership approve a 90-day healthcare operations vertical GTM test to restore efficient growth without prematurely pivoting the company?
- Hypotheses: healthcare urgency is higher; a narrower message can improve pipeline quality; compliance and implementation risk can be bounded by gates.
- MECE branches: demand signal, sales economics, implementation burden, product/compliance fit, channel feasibility.
- Alternatives: horizontal-only optimization, scoped vertical test, full healthcare pivot.
- Scenario variables: conversion quality, sales-cycle change, implementation effort, compliance blockers, sales-team distraction.
- Evidence that would change the answer: weak pain repeatability, long payback, blocking compliance requirements, no improvement in qualified pipeline.
- So what / now what: approve a reversible test with explicit scale/stop gates rather than funding a full pivot.

## Storyline

1. Growth is slowing, and broad horizontal optimization may not be enough.
2. Healthcare operations shows a directional urgency signal, but the evidence is not strong enough for a pivot.
3. The best move is a 90-day vertical GTM test with explicit stop/scale gates.
4. The test should validate segment pain, channel economics, implementation burden, and compliance blockers.
5. Leadership should approve a bounded test and reject both inaction and full pivot.
