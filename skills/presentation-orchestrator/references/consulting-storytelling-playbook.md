# Consulting Storytelling Playbook

Use this reference to create the consulting point of view before writing slide titles.

## Consulting POV Card

```markdown
Recommendation or hypothesis:
Why now:
Strategic logic:
Value at stake:
Options considered:
Rejected paths:
Tradeoffs:
Risks and mitigations:
Confidence:
What would change our mind:
Decision ask:
```

## Governing Thought

Write one sentence that can sit above the whole deck. It should combine:

- answer: what should the audience believe or approve?
- reason: why this is the right answer?
- stakes: why now or why it matters?

Good pattern: "We should [decision] because [core logic], while managing [main tradeoff]."

## SCQA Opening

Use SCQA when the audience needs fast orientation:

- Situation: shared context that the audience accepts.
- Complication: change, pressure, contradiction, or risk.
- Question: the decision now required.
- Answer: the recommendation or hypothesis.

Keep SCQA to one slide or opening section. Do not let it become a long background chapter.

## So What / Now What

For every claim, ask:

- So what: why does this matter to the audience's decision?
- Now what: what should they approve, stop, start, test, fund, or watch?

If a slide has no "now what", move it to appendix or delete it.

## Executive Summary Logic

Executive summary slides should include:

1. Recommendation.
2. Three reasons or proof points.
3. Decision ask.
4. Key risk and mitigation.
5. Evidence gap or next test when confidence is not high.

## Objection Handling

Before final output, name the likely objections:

- Evidence objection: "What proof supports this?"
- Tradeoff objection: "What are we giving up?"
- Execution objection: "Can we actually do this?"
- Timing objection: "Why now?"
- Financial objection: "What is the value at stake?"
- Strategic objection: "Why this path versus alternatives?"

Write a response or mark the gap explicitly.

## What Would Change Our Mind

Consulting decks are stronger when they show falsifiers. Add 1 to 3 conditions that would reverse or weaken the recommendation, such as:

- customer segment economics below threshold
- channel CAC above acceptable range
- sales cycle too long for payback target
- competitor response invalidates differentiation
- implementation capacity is unavailable
