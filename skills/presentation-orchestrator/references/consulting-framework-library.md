# Consulting Framework Library

Use these framework cards when selecting an analysis model. Frameworks are tools for judgment, not slide decoration.

## SWOT / TOWS

- Use when: the user starts with a broad situation scan or asks for SWOT.
- Inputs: internal strengths/weaknesses, external opportunities/threats, evidence for each.
- Slide pattern: diagnostic 2x2 plus TOWS action options.
- Trap: ending with SWOT instead of recommendation.
- Consulting question: "Which action becomes logical when strengths meet opportunities or threats expose weaknesses?"

## Porter Five Forces

- Use when: industry attractiveness, competitive pressure, supplier/buyer power, substitutes, or entry barriers matter.
- Inputs: competitor set, buyers, suppliers, substitutes, new entrants, switching costs, margin pressure.
- Slide pattern: force map with implication for attractiveness and strategy.
- Trap: drawing five boxes without saying how it changes the decision.
- Consulting question: "Is this market structurally attractive enough for the proposed move?"

## Value Chain

- Use when: competitive advantage, cost structure, capability gaps, or operating model choices matter.
- Inputs: primary activities, support activities, cost/value drivers, differentiating capabilities.
- Slide pattern: value chain with highlighted advantage or bottleneck.
- Trap: mapping activities without naming advantage or constraint.
- Consulting question: "Where do we create differentiated value, and where are we structurally weak?"

## BCG Matrix

- Use when: portfolio allocation across products, categories, segments, or markets matters.
- Inputs: relative share, market growth, investment needs, profitability or strategic value.
- Slide pattern: portfolio 2x2 with investment posture.
- Trap: using it for a single product with no portfolio choice.
- Consulting question: "Where should capital and attention shift?"

## Ansoff Matrix

- Use when: growth options need to be compared by product/market novelty.
- Inputs: existing/new products, existing/new markets, risk, capability fit, investment.
- Slide pattern: growth option 2x2 with recommended path.
- Trap: treating all growth moves as equal risk.
- Consulting question: "Which growth path has the best risk-adjusted fit?"

## McKinsey 7S

- Use when: transformation, operating model, or implementation alignment matters.
- Inputs: strategy, structure, systems, shared values, skills, style, staff.
- Slide pattern: 7S alignment map with gaps and actions.
- Trap: diagnosing culture when the decision is market strategy.
- Consulting question: "What must align internally for the recommendation to work?"

## Business Model Canvas

- Use when: new venture, GTM model, product strategy, monetization, or business model design matters.
- Inputs: customer segments, value propositions, channels, relationships, revenue, resources, activities, partners, costs.
- Slide pattern: canvas plus critical assumptions and tests.
- Trap: filling boxes without prioritizing the riskiest assumptions.
- Consulting question: "What business model must be true for this strategy to work?"

## Playing To Win Strategy Cascade

- Use when: the team must choose where to compete and how to win.
- Inputs: aspiration, where-to-play options, how-to-win logic, required capabilities, management systems.
- Slide pattern: strategy cascade plus tradeoffs.
- Trap: listing aspirations without tradeoffs.
- Consulting question: "What choices are we making, and what are we choosing not to do?"

## Issue Tree / MECE

- Use when: the problem is ambiguous and needs decomposition.
- Inputs: problem statement, candidate drivers, data availability, decision horizon.
- Slide pattern: issue tree with prioritized branches and evidence plan.
- Trap: exhaustive tree with no prioritization.
- Consulting question: "Which few branches are most likely to change the answer?"

## Option Comparison / Decision Table

- Use when: leaders must choose among routes.
- Inputs: options, decision criteria, evidence, risk, cost, time, upside, reversibility.
- Slide pattern: side-by-side comparison with recommendation and rejected paths.
- Trap: score theater with unsupported weights.
- Consulting question: "Which option wins under the criteria executives actually care about?"
