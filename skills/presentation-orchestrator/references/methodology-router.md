# Methodology Router

Use this reference after intake. Route by consulting decision state first, then by presentation format. Pick one primary framework and at most two support methods.

## Route By Decision State

| Decision state | Primary method | Support methods | Best output |
|---|---|---|---|
| Known recommendation | Pyramid Principle | Decision memo, evidence ledger | Answer-first executive decision deck |
| Uncertain hypothesis | Bulletproof Problem Solving | Issue tree, MECE, workplan | Problem-solving deck with tested options |
| Options tradeoff | Option comparison | Decision table, risk/return, sensitivity | Recommendation deck with rejected paths |
| Strategy/framework diagnosis | Key Management Models | Porter, SWOT/TOWS, BCG, Ansoff, 7S, value chain | Framework diagnosis plus strategic choice |
| Competitive advantage question | Porter/Magretta | Five Forces, value chain, tradeoffs | Competitive position deck |
| Where-to-play/how-to-win question | Playing to Win | Strategy cascade, capabilities, systems | Strategy choice deck |
| Growth or GTM question | Growth/GTM logic | Funnel, Traction/Bullseye, segment/channel tests | Growth investment or experiment deck |
| Business model/design question | Business Model Canvas | Value proposition, revenue/cost model, assumptions | Business model deck |
| Data readout | Data storytelling | Say It With Charts, appendix evidence | Insight deck with implications |
| Pitch or keynote | Persuasive arc | Resonate, slide:ology, Presentation Zen | Speaker-led persuasion deck |
| Training or workshop | ADDIE or workshop flow | Practice, facilitation, decision capture | Teaching deck or readout |

## Framework Fit Checks

- Pyramid Principle fits when the answer is known or can be stated as a hypothesis.
- Issue tree / MECE fits when the question must be decomposed before a recommendation is credible.
- SWOT alone is never enough; use TOWS or strategy kernel to turn it into actions.
- Porter fits when industry structure, competitive advantage, value chain, differentiation, or tradeoffs matter.
- BCG and Ansoff fit portfolio and growth-choice conversations; avoid them for single-product diagnosis unless portfolio logic matters.
- 7S fits operating model and transformation alignment, not market attractiveness.
- Business Model Canvas fits venture, product strategy, and monetization questions.
- Say It With Charts fits exhibit choice after the business message is known.

## Common Consulting Combinations

- Pyramid Principle + Porter: competitive recommendation.
- Pyramid Principle + Playing to Win: strategic choice deck.
- Issue tree + Bulletproof Problem Solving: ambiguous root-cause deck.
- Key Management Models + TOWS: user starts with SWOT and needs action options.
- Business Model Canvas + option comparison: new venture or GTM model decision.
- Growth experiments + Traction: acquisition/channel investment decision.
- Say It With Charts + evidence ledger: data-heavy executive readout.

## Anti-Patterns

- Framework pileup: more models do not mean better thinking.
- SWOT as final answer: make it a diagnosis input, then choose actions.
- Pretty deck before POV: visual polish cannot fix a missing recommendation.
- Data dump: every chart needs a written implication and decision relevance.
- Fake certainty: preserve evidence gaps and confidence level.
