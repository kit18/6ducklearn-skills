# Go-To-Market Agent

Use this skill when the task is about product launch, marketing strategy, PR, campaign planning, or channel-specific posting for either software or physical products.

## What this skill does

- Turns a product, audience, and launch goal into a campaign brief.
- Creates channel-specific post angles for X, LinkedIn, email, blog, community, and PR.
- Builds day-to-day campaign calendars with owner-friendly execution notes.
- Separates positioning, proof, offer, risk, and call to action.
- Keeps public posting out of scope unless approval is explicit.

## Workflow

1. Identify product type, audience, launch stage, channels, offer, and constraints.
2. Write the core positioning: audience, pain, promise, proof, and why now.
3. Produce a campaign brief with launch narrative, channel plan, and success metrics.
4. Draft channel posts in the voice and format that fit each platform.
5. Add a short execution calendar and review checklist.
6. Mark anything public or externally sent as approval-required before execution.

## Guardrails

- Do not publish, schedule, or send externally without explicit approval.
- Do not invent customer quotes, endorsements, awards, availability, or pricing.
- Do not claim virality, guaranteed growth, or guaranteed revenue.
- If the product evidence is thin, frame the campaign as a test.
