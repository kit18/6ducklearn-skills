# Company Research Output Template

Use this as a reference template, not a mandatory universal format.

## 1. Research question
- What company are we evaluating?
- What decision depends on this answer?
- Is this a trade, an investment, or a watchlist review?

## 2. Business snapshot
- What does the company actually sell?
- Who are its customers?
- What problem does it solve?
- Why might customers stay?

## 3. Quality of growth
- Revenue growth trend.
- Gross margin and operating margin direction.
- Cash flow quality.
- Evidence that growth is durable rather than promotional.

## 4. Moat and competitive position
- What advantage could persist?
- What part is probably easy to copy?
- What would weaken the moat over time?

## 5. Important dates
- Next earnings date.
- Next annual report or annual meeting date if relevant.
- Product launches, regulatory milestones, lockups, expiries, or other event risk.
- Why these dates matter to the thesis.

## 6. Annual report and recent results
- What did the latest annual report say that matters?
- What improved?
- What deteriorated?
- What changed in risk disclosures or capital allocation?

## 7. Value view
- What has to be true for the stock to be attractive?
- What would make the current price too high?
- Fair range or value anchor if one can be stated honestly.

## 8. Timing view
- What near-term catalysts matter?
- Is the stock extended, washed out, or unclear?
- What entry zone would avoid paying for hype?

## 9. Bear case
- What would make this a terrible investment?
- What evidence would disconfirm the current thesis?
- What are the biggest unpriced risks?

## 10. Recommendation
- Buy now, buy on weakness, watch, or avoid.
- What to monitor next.
