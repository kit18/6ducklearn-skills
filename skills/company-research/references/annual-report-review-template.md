# Annual Report Review Template

Use the annual report to understand the business, not just to confirm the story you want.

## 1. Revenue and segment quality
- Which segments are growing?
- Which segments are slowing or becoming lower quality?
- How concentrated is revenue?

## 2. Margins and returns
- Gross margin trend.
- Operating margin trend.
- Return on capital or return on equity if useful.
- Signs of pricing power versus cost pressure.

## 3. Cash flow and balance sheet
- Free cash flow trend.
- Working capital behavior.
- Debt load and refinancing risk.
- Share issuance, dilution, buybacks, and dividends.

## 4. Management signals
- Capital allocation discipline.
- Risk disclosures that expanded or became sharper.
- Dependence on adjustments or optimistic framing.

## 5. What changed materially
- New strategic emphasis.
- Shrinking moat signals.
- Customer concentration changes.
- Regulatory, litigation, or geopolitical exposure.

## 6. Bottom line
- What got stronger?
- What got weaker?
- What would make the annual report a warning sign rather than a comfort signal?
